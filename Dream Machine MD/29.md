Editors Pick- Is Sony going all in on AI for
Games?
Sony is increasingly positioning AI as a core part of how PlayStation
games are made, not just an experimental add-on. Across recent
updates from its game leadership, the company has made it clear this
week that AI will be embedded directly into development pipelines
across its first-party studios, with the goal of speeding up and enhancing
production and expanding creative output.
Rather than framing AI as a replacement for developers, Sony describes
it as a force multiplier for creativity. Internal tools are already being used
for tasks like animation support, QA automation, asset generation, and
engineering assistance. Some systems are reportedly able to convert
performance capture into animated facial models in seconds, reducing
what used to take hours of manual process work.
The broader strategic bet is straightforward: if AI reduces the cost and
time of making games, studios can build more of them. Sony expects
this shift to increase both the volume and variety of releases across
PlayStation, while also improving discovery through AI-driven
recommendations and personalisation systems inside the platform itself.
Taken together, this marks a quiet but significant shift — AI is becoming
part of the infrastructure of game development. Not a feature layered on
top of games, but a set of systems shaping how they are built, scaled,
and distributed across the entire PlayStation ecosystem.
