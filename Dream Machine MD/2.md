Editors Pick
OpenAI released its agents this week, but what could that mean for the
Creative Industries?
Agentic AI—the class of AI systems that can plan, act, and pursue goals
with autonomy—promises a new era of collaboration in creative
industries. Unlike passive “prompt-and-respond” tools, agentic systems
can assume roles: a design companion, an editing assistant, a rights
manager, a researcher for a newsletter ( 🧐 ) or a workflow orchestrator.

Human-AI Agency Continuum.
In creative workflows, agentic AI can handle routine but essential tasks—
asset cataloguing, clip indexing, rough cuts, rote research or metadata
tagging—so that potentially human creators stay in the high-imagination
zone, the human bit. At the same time, agents can propose variations,
monitor aesthetic consistency, and flag ethical or attribution issues.
Because creative work thrives on iteration, surprise, and feedback,
agentic AI becomes a tool within the loop and potentially helps with
getting more loops/iterations into the process, which could be a positive
thing: it suggests, adapts, and learns, while the human remains the
ultimate arbiter of taste, meaning, nuance and cultural value.
That said, the path forward isnʼt without tension. Questions around
authorship, attribution, fairness, and the balance of control are very real
in an agentic AI era. The creative industries must experiment, define best
practices, and embed safeguards, consider the implications of a more
systems based media world to ensure that agentic AI remains a
collaborator—and not a “black box” usurper. [Here's another article](https://www.tvbeurope.com/artificial-intelligence/opinion-is-agentic-ai-about-to-change-the-media-and-entertainment-industry)
