OpenAI last week unveiled [Sora 2](https://openai.com/index/sora-2/) , described as a major leap in AI video
and audio generation. The model creates highly realistic, physically
accurate scenes—with true-to-life motion, synchronized sound, and
even “cameos” of real people or objects.
Now rolling out via invite-only iOS app in the U.S. and Canada, Sora 2
marks a new frontier in co-creative storytelling and generative media.
But it's already been plagued with [copyright](https://www.nbcnews.com/tech/tech-news/openai-sora-2-app-video-chatgpt-creation-rcna234973) and [guardrail](https://www.theguardian.com/us-news/2025/oct/04/openai-sora-violence-racism) concerns...
as well as several dubious celebrity videos appearing on social media
over the weekend, sparking concerns that [it is time social media will](https://www.wuft.org/2025-10-03/kiss-reality-goodbye-ai-generated-social-media-has-arrived)
[kiss reality goodbye](https://www.wuft.org/2025-10-03/kiss-reality-goodbye-ai-generated-social-media-has-arrived) . Its time to take AI seriously.
