[A A first first look look at at ROLI's ROLI's AI AI Music Music Coach Coach](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=dbzq1lcqkDQ)
ROLI has unveiled AI Music Coach, a new feature inside the ROLI Learn
app that uses conversational AI and real-time performance analysis to
guide piano practice — and itʼs launching first as a closed beta for
Airwave owners, with a public beta pencilled in for late March 2026.
AI Music Coach is “unlocked” by Airwave , ROLIʼs infrared hand-tracking
device, which the company says tracks 27 joints in each hand at 90
frames per second so it can respond to technique (posture, movement,
fingering), not just note accuracy. That data feeds into live, visual
feedback inside the app, while the “coach” layer runs as a voice-driven
back-and-forth — essentially turning practice into a dialogue rather than
a one-way lesson.
Early hands-on reports frame it as a bid to lower the barrier to learning:
targeted prompts (e.g., switching keys, isolating weak fingers, adjusting
tempo), adaptive exercises, and more playful modes designed to keep
beginners engaged — including game-like learning experiences
highlighted in previews. The message from CEO Roland Lamb in demos
is that this is meant to augment , not replace, human teachers by
offering granular, always-available feedback that a weekly lesson canʼt
always capture.
There are also the usual “first beta” caveats: reviewers noted occasional
hiccups in voice interaction and stability during demos, but the broader
direction is clear — ROLI is placing AI on the practice side of music tech,
focused on coaching and habit-building. If the rollout lands, it could set a
new baseline for what “smart instruments” mean: not just sensors and
lights, but a responsive tutor and motivation built into the learning loop.
