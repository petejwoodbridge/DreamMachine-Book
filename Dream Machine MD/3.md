Editors Pick
Veo 3.1 and the Rise of AI Filmmaking
Googleʼs latest update to its AI video model, Veo 3.1 , pushes its Flow
filmmaking tool closer to true creative production. The new version
brings smoother scene transitions, better visual consistency, and more
precise control—letting creators extend shots, guide style and motion
with reference images, and tweak moments without jumping into another
editor. Itʼs less about one-off clips now, and more about
building coherent sequences directly inside an AI environment.
This marks a shift from prompt-to-video gimmicks to genuine AI-
assisted storytelling . Flow is evolving into an authoring space where
creatives can prototype ideas at cinematic scale—fast, iterative, and
expressive. Itʼs still experimental, but the message is clear: the next wave
of filmmaking wonʼt be about replacing directors, itʼll be about
reprogramming how we imagine and assemble moving stories.
