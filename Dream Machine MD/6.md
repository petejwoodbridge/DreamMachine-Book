86 % of creators in the survey now use generative AI in their
workflows
76 % say itʼs helped grow their business or brand
81 % say AI lets them make content they couldnʼt before
60 % use more than one AI tool regularly
Top uses: editing/enhancement (55 %) , asset generation (52
%) , ideation (48 %)
69 % worry about their work being used to train AI without consent
Barriers: cost ( 38 % ), unreliable quality ( 34 % ), transparency ( 28
% )
85 % would use AI that learns their creative style
70 % are optimistic about Agentic AI (tools that act on their behalf)
72 % already create on mobile; 75 % expect to do more next year
What it means
Generative AI has crossed the threshold — from optional to essential.
Creators now see it as a growth engine, not just an experiment. But trust
remains fragile: most still worry about ownership, consent, and how their
style or data is used.
The next frontier
Agentic AI — wanting to degine systems that learn and act in your
creative style — is emerging fast. 85 % of respondents said they want AI
that adapts to them, not the other way around . This points toward a
future of personalised style-agents, adaptive 'authored' workflows
and creative co-pilots , not just generic tools.
Why it matters
For creative studios, musicians, filmmakers, and designers— the
message is clear: AI fluency is becoming an essential part of creative
literacy. The opportunity is not to resist automation, but to build the
frameworks that preserve provenance, authorship, and human intent in
the age of infinite creation.
