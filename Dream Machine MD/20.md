The range is striking. Just this week, organisations
like BBC , Amazon , Google , UniversalMusic Group , Tencent and DNEG
are all now hiring for roles that sit at the intersection of AI, production
and creativity .
And is AI going to be an integral part of every role in the future? LinkedIn
reported a 21× increase in job postings mentioning generative AI in
2024 , while demand for AI skills across all job categories has grown
by over 70% globally since 2022 . The World Economic Forum also
estimates AI and machine learning specialists will be among the
fastest-growing roles this decade , with millions of new positions
emerging worldwide.
Whatʼs changing isnʼt just the number of jobs — itʼs the types of roles
emerging . Studios are hiring AI artists and model specialists . Game
companies are recruiting ai focusedtechnical artists and engineers .
Music companies are building teams around AI strategy, tools and
experimentation .
At the centre of this shift is a hybrid role appearing across the sector: the
always adaptable Creative Technologists — who sit between art,
engineering and storytelling, building prototypes and rethinking
production workflows. The message across the creative industry is
becoming clear: AI literacy is rapidly becoming part of many job
descriptions.

roles currently open across the creative AI ecosystem (mostly in UK
