The Internet we once knew — a messy, human-driven web of creativity,
curiosity, and connection — is quietly being replaced by something else.
According to new data from Graphite (2025) , more than half of all new
online content is already generated by AI. The surge began after the
release of large language models in 2022, accelerating through 2024
before stabilizing at a 50–50 human–machine split. But while the
machines are writing, theyʼre also reading — and increasingly, theyʼre
reading only each other.
Machine Traffic, Machine Readers
51% of global web traffic now comes from bots. 79% of
all bot activity is for AI training. [Cloudflare / Fastly /](https://blog.cloudflare.com/crawlers-click-ai-bots-training/)
[Imperva, 2025](https://blog.cloudflare.com/crawlers-click-ai-bots-training/)
Reports from major infrastructure providers show that bots have now
overtaken people as the Internetʼs main inhabitants. Cloudflare, Fastly,
and Imperva all confirm that automated systems generate over half of
all web traffic , with just one in five visits made by real humans. AI
crawlers like GPTBot, ClaudeBot, and Metaʼs scrapers dominate,
endlessly trawling for data to feed new models. The result: a recursive,
self-consuming loop of AI systems reading, rewriting, and re-indexing
each otherʼs output — a synthetic mirror world made by and for
machines.
A Synthetic Economy
The generative content market is projected to hit $80 B
by 2030 . [Grand View Research, 2025](https://www.grandviewresearch.com/industry-analysis/generative-ai-content-creation-market-report)
Gartner and Europol forecast that 90–99% of online content could be
AI-generated or AI-assisted by 2030. The $14.8 billion generative-AI
content market (2024) is projected to quintuple within five years, driven
by automation in marketing, entertainment, and publishing. Yet as AI
consumes the Internet, researchers warn of “synthetic contamination” —
models trained on their own synthetic outputs, losing touch with real
human language, knowledge, and culture.
The Dead Internet — or Just a Different One?
If the early Internet was built on human attention , todayʼs version runs
on machine attention — bots crawling bots, LLMs summarising LLMs, AI
systems endlessly feeding each otherʼs loops. The “ [dead Internet](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Dead_Internet_theory) ” isnʼt
a conspiracy theory; itʼs the logical endpoint of efficiency, automation,

What Survives the Shift
In this new ecology, authenticity and provenance become the new
scarcity. When everything online can be faked, cloned, or generated at
near zero cost, proof that someone real made something becomes the
most valuable signal of all .
The Internet isnʼt quite dead, — but the human web inside it might be
changing drastically as we know it. This is an interesting [experiment](https://futurism.com/social-network-ai-intervention-echo-chamber)
[from dutch researchers](https://futurism.com/social-network-ai-intervention-echo-chamber) who built a social platform stripped to bare
essentials using AI agents. The bots fractured into warring tribes within

flourished. Maybe it's the architecture thats the problem?
